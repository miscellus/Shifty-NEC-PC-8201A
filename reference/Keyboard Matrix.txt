Keyboard Matrix

KB0	KB1	KB2	KB3	KB4	KB5	KB6	KB7
----------------------------------------------------------------+
Z	X	C	V	B	N	M	L	| PA0
A	S	D	F	G	H	J	K	| PA1
Q	W	E	R	T	Y	U	I	| PA2
O	P	@	\	,	.	/	]	| PA3
1	2	3	4	5	6	7	8	| PA4
9	0	;	:	-	[	Space	Insert	| PA5
BacSpc	Up	Down	Left	Right	Tab	Esc	Return	| PA6
F1	F2	F3	F4	F5	N/A	N/A	Stop	| PA7
Shift	Ctrl	Graph	N/A	Caps	N/A	N/A	N/A	| PB0


The keyboard is strobed in software.

It works as follows:
	1. Strobe is turned on for a keyboard row. E.g. To strobe the third row (the one starting QWERTY), out (1 << 2) to output port PORTA
	2. while the strobe is turned on, read a byte from KEYIN port. the LSB will be 0 if the Q key is pressed, otherwise the LSB will be 1 if Q is NOT pressed. the second least significant bit encodes the state of W, the third E, and so on.
	3. Turn off strobe.

Here is a routine for checking if SHIFT + STOP is pressed:

CheckStopKey:
	push b
	in PortB
	mov b,a
	ori 0b00000001
	out PortB
	in PortA
	mov c,a
	mvi a, 0b01111111
	out PortA
	in PortKeyScan
	push psw
	mvi a, 0b11111111
	out PortA
	mov a,b
	ani 0b11111110
	out PortB
	in PortKeyScan
	rrc
	mov a,c
	out PortA
	mov a,b
	out PortB
	pop b
	mov a,b
	rar
	ani 0b11000000
	pop b
	rnz
	inr a
	stc
	ret
